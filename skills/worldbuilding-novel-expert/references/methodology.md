# Methodology Reference

## World Rules Before Plot

A story world starts with constraints, not decoration. Extract the governing
rule, the cost of using or violating it, the institution that benefits from it,
and the forbidden pressure point that creates story motion.

## Character Choice With Cost

The protagonist should not merely witness the world. They must choose, and the
choice must leave a trace. Track desire, fear, agency, cost, and final change.

## Scene Pressure Ladder

Use a compact escalation pattern:

1. arrival under constraint
2. discovery that changes the rules
3. alliance, betrayal, or bargain
4. institutional pressure
5. final choice and consequence

Every scene should change at least one axis: knowledge, danger, relationship,
status, location, or moral obligation.

## Complete Novel Standard

Fail outputs that are only outlines, teaser openings, chapter previews,
fragments, or endings that promise the real story later. A valid short novel has
an opening image, pressure development, irreversible choice, cost, and resolved
arc.

## Continuity State

Keep stable references for character names, visual identities, locations, props,
costs, unresolved promises, and final consequences. Continuity should be treated
as data, not memory.

## Repair Heuristics

- If the world feels generic, add one visible rule and one material consequence.
- If the protagonist is passive, force a choice that closes one path.
- If the ending is vague, show a concrete changed object, relationship, or rule.
- If prose feels inflated, replace abstract claims with observed action.
- If continuity drifts, restate the stable IDs and rewrite only the drifting passages.
